Local-first authorship, not just local keys

Most Nostr clients are local-first in one important way: your identity and keys stay on your device.

But the writing itself is still tied to posting. In most apps, your note does not really exist until you publish it.

Continuum is local-first in a different way. Your writing exists locally as soon as you sign it — even if you never publish it, and even without an internet connection.

That changes the workflow completely:

  • you can write and sign without an internet connection
  • you can revise by signing a new version
  • you can decide later whether something becomes public

Your drafts, identities, signed notes, and full archive of your work live together in a workspace you control.

This archive is stored locally on your machine — not on a hosted platform or external service.

Who it’s for

Continuum is for people who want control over what they write and publish.

If you are happy using Word, Google Docs, Substack, or a standard Nostr client and do not think much about where your work lives, you probably do not need Continuum.

But if you are tired of splitting your work across documents, apps, browsers, identities, and platforms, and want everything in one place that you can run and keep yourself, Continuum will make sense.
